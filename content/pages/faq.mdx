faqCategories:
  - name: "Filing"
    items:
      - question: "What filings does DormantFile handle?"
        answer: "We handle two filings for dormant companies: annual dormant accounts submitted to Companies House and a nil CT600 Corporation Tax return submitted to HMRC. Both confirm your company was dormant during the accounting period."
      - question: "What if my company isn't registered for Corporation Tax?"
        answer: "That's fine. Many dormant companies only need to file annual accounts with Companies House. If yours isn't registered for Corporation Tax, you can skip the CT600 entirely. We handle both scenarios."
      - question: "Can I use DormantFile if my company is trading?"
        answer: "No. DormantFile is designed exclusively for genuinely dormant companies — those with no significant transactions during the period. If your company has traded, you need a qualified accountant."
      - question: "What do I need before I start?"
        answer: "For Companies House accounts: your company registration number and Companies House authentication code. For the CT600: your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) and HMRC Government Gateway credentials (user ID and password). You will also need your accounting period dates for both filings."
      - question: "How long does filing take?"
        answer: "Under two minutes. You click file, enter the relevant credentials, and we handle the rest."
      - question: "What happens after I file?"
        answer: "Companies House and HMRC process your submission. Once accepted, we show the confirmation in your dashboard and send you an email. Your filing records are kept so you have a permanent history."
      - question: "What accounting periods can I file for?"
        answer: "You can file for any accounting period where your company was dormant. We calculate your deadlines based on the period dates you enter."
      - question: "What if I'm behind on my filings?"
        answer: "DormantFile lets you file for any outstanding period. If your company has missed several years, you can work through each period from your dashboard. We recommend filing the oldest period first, and we'll warn you if earlier periods are still outstanding. See our guide on [catching up on overdue filings](/guides/catch-up-overdue-dormant-company-filings)."
      - question: "Can I use DormantFile if my company has a Bounce Back Loan?"
        answer: "Yes, as long as the company is genuinely dormant. A Bounce Back Loan is a creditor obligation — it does not affect your company's dormant status under the Companies Act (provided repayments are not made through the company's own bank account). You still need to file annually while the loan is outstanding. See our [guide on BBLs and dormant companies](/guides/bounce-back-loan-dormant-company)."
  - name: "Security"
    items:
      - question: "Is my data secure?"
        answer: "Yes. All data is transmitted over TLS encryption. Your DormantFile password is hashed with bcrypt. Payment is handled by Stripe — we never see your card details. Read our security page for full details."
      - question: "How do you handle my HMRC Gateway credentials?"
        answer: "Your HMRC Government Gateway user ID and password are used once during the CT600 submission. They are transmitted to HMRC over TLS, never stored in our database, and discarded from server memory immediately after HMRC responds."
      - question: "How do you handle my Companies House authentication code?"
        answer: "Your Companies House authentication code is entered at the point of filing and transmitted directly to Companies House over TLS. We do not store it in our database."
      - question: "What data do you collect?"
        answer: "We collect your email address, a hashed password, company details (name, registration number, UTR, accounting dates), and filing records. Payment is handled entirely by Stripe — we never see or store your card details."
  - name: "Pricing"
    items:
      - question: "How much does it cost?"
        answer: "Basic is £19/year for one company. Multiple is £39/year for up to 10 companies. Agent is £49/year for up to 100 companies. Plans are based on how many companies you manage — not how many filings you make. All plans include both Companies House accounts and CT600 filing for any outstanding period. See our pricing page for the full comparison."
      - question: "Is there a free trial?"
        answer: "We don't currently offer a free trial, but the Basic plan is just £19/year — less than what most accountants charge for a single nil filing."
      - question: "Can I cancel anytime?"
        answer: "Yes. Cancel via the billing portal and your subscription stays active until the end of the current billing period. We do not offer refunds for partial periods — see our [refund policy](/refund) for details."
      - question: "What happens if my subscription lapses?"
        answer: "You won't be able to file new returns, but your account and filing history remain accessible. You can resubscribe at any time to resume filing."
  - name: "Account"
    items:
      - question: "Can I manage multiple companies?"
        answer: "Yes, on the Multiple or Agent plan. Each company has its own filing record, deadlines, and reminders. You manage them all from a single dashboard."
      - question: "When do I get filing reminders?"
        answer: "We send email reminders at 90, 30, 14, 7, 3, and 1 days before each deadline so you never miss a filing date."
      - question: "How do I add or remove a company?"
        answer: "Add a company from your dashboard by entering its registration number. Remove a company from your settings — the filing history is preserved."
      - question: "How do I delete my account?"
        answer: "You can delete your account from the settings page. This removes all your personal data and company records. This action is permanent and cannot be undone."
