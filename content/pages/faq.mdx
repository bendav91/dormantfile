faqCategories:
  - name: "Filing"
    items:
      - question: "What filings does DormantFile handle?"
        answer: "DormantFile handles both annual filing obligations for UK dormant companies. We submit dormant company accounts to Companies House via the Software Filing API and nil CT600 Corporation Tax returns to HMRC via the GovTalk API. Both filings are submitted electronically — you never need to post anything. Together, these cover the complete annual filing obligation for a dormant limited company. Each filing is tracked separately in your dashboard, so you can see the status of your Companies House accounts and CT600 independently."
      - question: "What if my company isn't registered for Corporation Tax?"
        answer: "That's fine. Many dormant companies only need to file annual accounts with Companies House. If yours isn't registered for Corporation Tax, you can skip the CT600 entirely. We handle both scenarios."
      - question: "Can I use DormantFile if my company is trading?"
        answer: "No. DormantFile is designed exclusively for genuinely dormant companies — those with no significant transactions during the period. If your company has traded, you need a qualified accountant."
      - question: "What do I need before I start?"
        answer: "For Companies House accounts: your company registration number and Companies House authentication code. For the CT600: your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) and HMRC Government Gateway credentials (user ID and password). You will also need your accounting period dates for both filings."
      - question: "How long does filing take?"
        answer: "Under two minutes. You click file, enter the relevant credentials, and we handle the rest."
      - question: "What happens after I file?"
        answer: "After you submit, DormantFile polls the government APIs for a response. Companies House typically responds within minutes; HMRC can take several hours or occasionally longer. You'll see the status update in your dashboard — it moves from submitted to accepted or rejected. Once a filing is accepted, you'll receive an email confirmation. If a filing is rejected, you'll see the reason in your dashboard and can correct the issue and resubmit. Your filing records are kept permanently so you always have a complete history."
      - question: "What accounting periods can I file for?"
        answer: "You can file for any accounting period where your company was dormant. We calculate your deadlines based on the period dates you enter."
      - question: "What if I'm behind on my filings?"
        answer: "DormantFile detects outstanding accounting periods and lets you file them in sequence. Companies House and HMRC track periods independently, so you can catch up on accounts and Corporation Tax separately. We recommend filing the oldest period first, and we'll warn you if earlier periods are still outstanding. Late filing penalties may apply from Companies House — these escalate from £150 for up to one month late. Filing now prevents further penalties from accruing. See our guide on [catching up on overdue filings](/guides/catch-up-overdue-dormant-company-filings)."
      - question: "Can I use DormantFile if my company has a Bounce Back Loan?"
        answer: "A Bounce Back Loan doesn't automatically make your company non-dormant for Companies Act purposes. The key test is whether there have been significant accounting transactions beyond shares paid for, filing fees, and penalties. However, HMRC may take a different view if interest has accrued or repayments are being made through the company's own bank account. If you're unsure, check with HMRC or an accountant before filing as dormant. You still need to file annually while the loan is outstanding. See our [guide on BBLs and dormant companies](/guides/bounce-back-loan-dormant-company)."
      - question: "What happens if HMRC or Companies House rejects my filing?"
        answer: "Rejections are uncommon for dormant filings but can happen if credentials are incorrect, the company isn't registered for Corporation Tax, or there's a period mismatch. DormantFile shows the rejection reason in your dashboard so you can correct and resubmit. If you're stuck, email support and we'll help you work out what went wrong."
      - question: "Can I amend a filing after it's been accepted?"
        answer: "Once a filing has been accepted by HMRC or Companies House, it cannot be amended through DormantFile. Amendments need to be filed separately through official channels. For dormant accounts, amendments are extremely rare since the figures are standardised — if your company was genuinely dormant, the original filing will be correct."
      - question: "Is this my first year — can I still use DormantFile?"
        answer: "Yes. First-year filings work the same way. DormantFile calculates your first accounting period based on your incorporation date and accounting reference date. If your company was incorporated within the current period, DormantFile handles the first-year filing correctly."
      - question: "My company has a bank account but no transactions — is it still dormant?"
        answer: "For Companies Act purposes, holding a bank account is fine as long as there have been no significant accounting transactions. The key test is whether the company has traded. If the only transactions are formation shares, filing fees, and penalties, the company is still dormant. Interest earned on the account could affect this — check with HMRC if in doubt."
      - question: "What happened to CATO?"
        answer: "HMRC's Corporation Tax Online (CATO) service closed on 31 March 2026. CATO was the free way to file a nil CT600 return directly with HMRC. With it gone, you need either approved software like DormantFile or an accountant to file your Corporation Tax return. Companies House accounts filing is unaffected — that's a separate system."
      - question: "Who can see the filings DormantFile submits?"
        answer: "Companies House filings (annual accounts) are public record — anyone can view them on the Companies House register, regardless of how they were filed. HMRC Corporation Tax returns are not public. DormantFile submits to the same official systems as any accountant or filing software."
  - name: "Security"
    items:
      - question: "Is my data secure?"
        answer: "Yes. All data is transmitted over TLS encryption in transit. Your DormantFile password is hashed with bcrypt before being stored — we never see or store it in plain text. Payments are handled entirely by Stripe, so your card details never touch our servers. Government credentials (HMRC Gateway, Companies House auth code) are used once at filing time and immediately discarded — they are never written to our database or logged. We use minimal cookies: one session cookie for authentication and an optional analytics cookie. You can read more on our [security page](/security)."
      - question: "How do you handle my HMRC Gateway credentials?"
        answer: "Your HMRC Government Gateway user ID and password are entered at filing time only. They are transmitted directly to HMRC over TLS to authenticate the CT600 submission. Once HMRC responds, they are immediately discarded from server memory. They are never written to our database, never logged, and never accessible to anyone at DormantFile. You can verify this in our [security policy](/security)."
      - question: "How do you handle my Companies House authentication code?"
        answer: "Your Companies House authentication code is entered when you file. It is included in the submission XML sent to Companies House, transmitted over TLS, and never stored in our database. The auth code is a one-time-use code issued by Companies House specifically for software filing. Like your HMRC credentials, it is discarded from server memory immediately after the submission completes and is never logged."
      - question: "What data do you collect?"
        answer: "We collect your email address, a hashed password, company details (name, registration number, UTR, accounting dates), and filing records. Payment is handled entirely by Stripe — we never see or store your card details."
